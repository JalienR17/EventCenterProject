Biography

 

Carlos Alcaraz is a Spanish professional tennis player who has quickly become one of the sport’s brightest stars. Born on May 5, 2003, in El Palmar, Spain, he turned professional in 2018 and has been hailed as the future of men’s tennis. With an aggressive playing style, incredible athleticism, and a fearless mentality, Alcaraz has drawn comparisons to his fellow Spaniard Rafael Nadal while forging his own unique path.

His breakthrough came in 2022 when he won his first Grand Slam at the US Open, becoming the youngest World No. 1 in ATP history. Since then, he has continued to thrive on all surfaces, showcasing his powerful forehand, remarkable speed, and ability to play with flair and confidence in high-pressure moments. Still early in his career, Alcaraz is expected to be a dominant force in men’s tennis for years to come. His combination of youth, skill, and competitive fire makes him a key figure in the sport’s next generation.
