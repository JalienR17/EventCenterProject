Biography

 

Coco Gauff is an American professional tennis player who has rapidly risen to stardom with her incredible athleticism, mental toughness, and all-court game. Born on March 13, 2004, in Delray Beach, Florida, she gained global attention at just 15 years old when she defeated Venus Williams at Wimbledon 2019.

Since then, Gauff has developed into one of the top players in women's tennis, winning her first Grand Slam title at the US Open in 2023. Known for her speed, powerful backhand, and relentless competitive spirit, she has become a dominant force on the WTA Tour. Gauff is also recognized for her poise, leadership, and advocacy off the court, making her one of the most influential young athletes in the sport. Gauff is not just a future star—she is already a major contender and a role model for the next generation. With her talent, determination, and continued improvement, she is expected to win multiple Grand Slams and lead the future of women’s tennis.
