About the Miami Open

 

 

The Miami Open is one of the most prestigious tennis tournaments in the world, held annually in Miami, Florida. Established in 1985, the tournament is part of both the ATP Tour Masters 1000 and the WTA 1000 series, attracting the biggest names in men's and women's tennis.

Since 2019, the Miami Open has been hosted at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens, offering a state-of-the-art venue with world-class facilities. The tournament is known for its vibrant atmosphere, blending elite tennis with the energy and culture of Miami, including live music, art, and culinary experiences.

As one of the key stops on the professional tennis circuit, the Miami Open serves as a crucial lead-up to the Grand Slam events, delivering thrilling matches and unforgettable moments for fans worldwide.

For more information, visit the official Miami Open website.
