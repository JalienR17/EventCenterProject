Biography

 

Novak Djokovic is a Serbian professional tennis player widely regarded as one of the greatest of all time. Born on May 22, 1987, in Belgrade, Serbia, he turned professional in 2003 and quickly rose through the ranks with his exceptional skill, endurance, and mental toughness. Djokovic is known for his all-court game, incredible flexibility, and unparalleled return of serve.

Over his career, he has dominated men’s tennis alongside Roger Federer and Rafael Nadal, forming the "Big Three." Djokovic holds numerous records, including the most weeks at No. 1 in the ATP rankings and the most Grand Slam titles in men's tennis history. His consistency, resilience, and ability to thrive under pressure have made him one of the sport’s most accomplished athletes. Djokovic continues to break records and redefine greatness, cementing his legacy as one of the most dominant athletes in tennis history.
